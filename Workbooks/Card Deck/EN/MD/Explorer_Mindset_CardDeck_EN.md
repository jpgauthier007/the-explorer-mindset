# THE EXPLORER MINDSET — CARD DECK
## 52 Weekly Explorer Prompts

*Jean-Philippe Gauthier · theexplorermindset.com*

> One card. One week. One small expedition at a time.

Use one card per week, or draw one whenever you need a nudge. Each card belongs to one of four suits that mirror the four pillars of the Explorer Mindset.

| Suit | Theme | Cards |
|------|-------|-------|
| CURIOSITY | Open your eyes to the world around you | 01–13 |
| UNKNOWN | Build your tolerance for uncertainty | 14–26 |
| RESILIENCE | Strengthen your capacity to recover | 27–39 |
| PATH | Discover and commit to your direction | 40–52 |

---

## CURIOSITY

*Open your eyes to the world around you.*

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 01
**The Beginner's Eye**

Pick something you do every single day. Look at it today as if it's the first time. Write down three things you've never noticed before.

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 02
**The Five Whys**

Choose something you believe or routinely do. Ask yourself why. Then ask why again. Keep going for five levels. Where do you end up?

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 03
**The Curious Conversation**

Have a conversation today with one goal: understand the other person's point of view. Ask questions. Don't debate or advise. Just listen and learn.

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 04
**Learn One New Thing**

Spend 20 minutes learning something you know nothing about. A random Wikipedia article, a documentary on an unfamiliar subject, a podcast outside your usual interests.

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 05
**The Question Collection**

Throughout the day, write down every question that crosses your mind, no matter how small or strange. Don't answer them yet. Just collect.

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 06
**Follow a Thread**

Take one question from yesterday and follow it. Search, read, ask someone. See where your curiosity leads when you give it permission to wander.

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 07
**The Expert's Blind Spot**

Find someone who is an expert in something you know nothing about. Ask them what most people misunderstand about their field. Listen without judgment.

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 08
**Borrow Someone's Lens**

Spend a day looking at your life through someone else's eyes. A child. A stranger. A future version of yourself. What do they notice that you've stopped seeing?

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 09
**The Uncomfortable Question**

What is a question you've been avoiding asking yourself? Write it down. You don't have to answer it today, just naming it is enough.

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 10
**Cross the Border**

Visit a place, space, or community you don't normally belong to. A different neighbourhood, a meetup outside your field, a genre of music you've never tried. What do you notice?

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 11
**The Assumption Hunt**

Pick one belief you hold about yourself, your work, or the world. Treat it as a hypothesis rather than a fact. What evidence would challenge it?

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 12
**Slow Down to Speed Up**

Choose one thing you usually rush through. Do it at half speed today. What do you notice when you're not in a hurry?

---

### CURIOSITY · Card 13
**The Wonder Log**

At the end of today, write down three things that genuinely surprised, delighted, or puzzled you. Make this a habit for one week and see what shifts.

---

## UNKNOWN

*Build your tolerance for uncertainty.*

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 14
**The Small Unknown**

Do one small thing differently today. Take a different route. Order something new. Sit somewhere else. Notice what it feels like to break a micro-routine.

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 15
**The Uncertainty Inventory**

List the uncertainties you're currently living with: things you don't know, can't control, or haven't decided yet. Just name them. Notice which feel heavy and which feel lighter once named.

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 16
**The Experiment**

Identify one small experiment you could run in your life. Something you've wondered about but haven't tested. What would it take to try it this week?

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 17
**Stay with the Unknown**

Choose one uncertainty from your inventory. Instead of trying to solve it today, just live with it. Write what it feels like not to know. What do you notice?

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 18
**Ask for Help**

Reach out to someone about something you don't understand. Admit what you don't know and ask for their perspective. Notice what it feels like to be openly uncertain.

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 19
**Run the Experiment**

Run the small experiment you identified. Don't focus on the outcome, focus on what you learn from trying.

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 20
**The Worst Case**

Think of something you've been avoiding because of fear. Write out the absolute worst case in detail. Then ask: how likely is it really? What would you do if it happened?

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 21
**The Best Case**

For that same fear, write out the best case scenario in equal detail. We rarely give the upside the same airtime as the downside. What do you notice?

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 22
**"I Don't Know"**

Today, every time you don't know something, say so out loud rather than guessing. Count how many times. Notice how people respond.

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 23
**The Pivot Rehearsal**

Think of something in your life that isn't working as planned. If you had to pivot right now, what would that look like? Write it out without judgment.

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 24
**The Pre-Mortem**

Pick a project or goal that matters to you. Imagine it's one year from now and it failed completely. What went wrong? Use this to anticipate real obstacles.

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 25
**First Principles**

Take something you've always done a certain way. Strip it to its fundamental assumptions. If you were starting from scratch with no rules, how would you do it differently?

---

### UNKNOWN · Card 26
**Embrace the Draft**

Create something today that you won't perfect. A rough sketch, a messy plan, an imperfect first attempt. Let go of finished. Notice what happens.

---

## RESILIENCE

*Strengthen your capacity to recover.*

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 27
**The Setback Story**

Recall a past setback that taught you something important. Was it "not yet," "wrong direction," or "external obstacle"? What did you take away from it?

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 28
**The Self-Compassion Practice**

Notice your inner critic today. When it speaks harshly, pause and ask: what would I say to a friend in this situation? Offer yourself that same kindness.

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 29
**The Debrief**

Think of a recent disappointment, large or small. Walk through the expedition debrief questions. What do you learn when you examine it systematically?

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 30
**The Stress Inventory**

What's causing you stress right now? List everything, then sort: what's within your control? What isn't? What is one action you can take on something you control?

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 31
**Seek Perspective**

Share something you're struggling with with a trusted person. Not to get advice, just to be heard. Notice what changes when you're not carrying it alone.

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 32
**Persist or Pivot?**

Is there something in your life where you're unsure whether to keep going or change direction? Sit with the persist-or-pivot questions. What clarity emerges?

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 33
**The Strength Inventory**

Write down 10 moments in your life where you showed real strength, resilience, or courage. Read them back. What pattern do you notice about who you are?

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 34
**The Recovery Ritual**

What do you do when you're at your lowest? Design a personal recovery ritual: three to five actions that reliably help you reset. Write it down so it's ready when you need it.

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 35
**Failure as Data**

Think of something you've tried and failed at. Evaluate it as a scientist would: what data did you collect? What would you test differently next time?

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 36
**The Comparison Trap**

Notice every time you compare yourself to someone else today. Just observe without judgment. Where is comparison energizing you, and where is it draining you?

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 37
**The Gratitude Anchor**

Write down three things from the past week you're genuinely grateful for, not the usual ones: specific, small moments. Notice how this reframes your recent experience.

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 38
**Energy Audit**

Track your energy across the day today. What activities drain you? What restores you? Where are you spending energy on things that don't deserve it?

---

### RESILIENCE · Card 39
**The Long Game**

Think of a challenge you're facing right now. Write about it from the perspective of your 80-year-old self. What does that version of you say about it?

---

## PATH

*Discover and commit to your direction.*

---

### PATH · Card 40
**The Values Excavation**

List 5 to 10 moments in your life when you felt most alive and aligned. What was present in those moments? What values or needs were being honored?

---

### PATH · Card 41
**The Anti-Vision**

Describe what you absolutely do not want your life to look like in five years. Sometimes clarity on what to avoid reveals what to pursue.

---

### PATH · Card 42
**The Brainstorm**

Give yourself 15 minutes to brainstorm paths, projects, or directions you could explore, with no judgment. Quantity over quality.

---

### PATH · Card 43
**A Conversation**

Identify someone who lives a life or does work that intrigues you. Reach out and ask if they'd share their story. Even if they say no, you've practiced asking.

---

### PATH · Card 44
**The Next Expedition**

Based on everything you've been exploring, name one small expedition you want to undertake next. Not a life plan, just a next step. Write it down clearly.

---

### PATH · Card 45
**Map the Obstacles**

What might get in the way of your next expedition? Name the obstacles, internal and external. For each one, imagine one way around it.

---

### PATH · Card 46
**The Commitment Ritual**

Create a small ritual to mark your commitment to your next expedition. Write a letter to yourself, tell someone who will hold you to it, or say your intention out loud.

---

### PATH · Card 47
**The Integration**

Look back over the last three months of your life. What patterns do you notice? What surprised you? What do you want to carry forward?

---

### PATH · Card 48
**Who Are You Becoming?**

Not who you are now, but who are you becoming? Write about the version of yourself you're growing toward. What does that person believe, do, and refuse to do?

---

### PATH · Card 49
**The Contribution Question**

What is the specific thing that only you can offer the world, given your unique combination of experiences, skills, and perspective? Write without modesty.

---

### PATH · Card 50
**The Decision on the Table**

Is there a decision you've been putting off? Write out every option clearly. Then ask: which option would the most courageous version of me choose?

---

### PATH · Card 51
**The Mentor You Need**

Who is the person you most need right now, a mentor, a peer, a challenger? Write a description of them. Then ask: where might that person already exist in your world?

---

### PATH · Card 52
**The Letter Forward**

Write a letter to yourself one year from now. Describe the expeditions you've taken, the things you've let go of, and the explorer you've become. Seal it and open it in twelve months.
